Our Story

From Ruins to Glory

Born in 1951 as "Məhsul" in the city of Ağdam, deep in the Karabakh region of Azerbaijan, this club's journey is unlike any other in world football. Renamed Qarabağ FK in 1987, the club won the Soviet-era Azerbaijan SSR Championship in 1988 — a sign of things to come.

Then war came. On 23 July 1993, during the First Nagorno-Karabakh War, Ağdam was seized and the team was forced to abandon their beloved Imarat Stadium — which was subsequently destroyed. Head coach and player Allahverdi Baghirov heroically perished in the conflict. Yet remarkably, in that same year of 1993, Qarabağ won both the Top League and the Azerbaijan Cup — a triumph of the human spirit against all odds.

"We play for a city we cannot return to. Every trophy is for Ağdam, for the people who had to leave, and for the ones who never came back."

For a decade, financial hardship plagued the displaced club. But in 2001, Azersun Holding stepped in as sponsor, providing the stability to rebuild. In 2008, former Azerbaijani football star Gurban Gurbanov took over as head coach — and changed everything.

What followed was a dynasty. Beginning in 2013–14, Qarabağ went on to become the most dominant force in Azerbaijani football history, claiming league title after league title, and making history in European competition. In 2017–18, the club became the first Azerbaijani team to reach the UEFA Champions League group stage — where they faced the likes of Atlético Madrid, Chelsea, and Roma.

On 27 April 2025, Qarabağ secured their 12th league title, their fourth in a row — a dynasty cemented. The club stands today as a symbol of resilience, identity, and Azerbaijani football excellence.

Gurban Gurbanov

Head Coach — The Architect of Success

Appointed in 2008, Gurban Gurbanov is the longest-serving and most successful manager in Azerbaijani football history. He created a unique playing style inspired by positional play, known as the "Qarabağ style." Under his leadership, the club transformed into a respected European competitor and Azerbaijani powerhouse.
